Leeks

Did you know?

Leeks are a member of the allium family along with onions and garlic

Grow:

Leeks are best suited for growing in the open ground in moist, light seedbeds. They can be grown though in large, deep containers or raised beds. They should be planted in early to mid spring

Water: These plants should be kept well watered, especially as they are initially becoming established.

Food: Feed regularly with a general purpose feed

Harvesting: Leeks should be harvested from mid autumn through to the end of late spring, depending on the time of sowing and the variety. They should be dug out using a spade or fork

Bonus:

The white and green parts of leeks taste different too so pack a two-taste punch to winter meals.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 3 large leeks
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 whole egg
  • 1 Mediterranean pie crust
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 3 ounces Gruyère, grated
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
Method:
  1. Cut away the root and dark green leaves from the leeks and cut in half lengthwise. Run under cold water to remove sand. Drain on paper towels.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Heat the oil or oil and butter over medium heat in a lidded skillet or saucepan and add the leeks and a pinch of salt. Cook gently, stirring, until they begin to soften. Turn the heat to medium-low, cover and cook gently until the leeks are very soft but not browned, stirring often. Stir in the garlic if using.
  3. Beat together the egg yolks and egg in a medium bowl. Set the tart pan on a baking sheet to allow for easy handling. Using a pastry brush lightly brush the bottom of the crust and place in the oven for 10 minutes.
  4. Add salt (about 1/2 teaspoon), pepper and the milk to the eggs and whisk together. Spread the leeks in an even layer in the crust. Sprinkle the cheese in an even layer on top. Pour in the custard filling. Place in the oven and bake for 30 minutes, or until set and just beginning to color on the top.
  5. Remove from the oven and allow to sit for at least 15 minutes before serving.
Source: NY Times

Fun fact:

Leeks are one of the national symbols of Wales. Legend says on the eve of the battle against the Saxons, Saint David advised the Britons to wear leeks in their caps so as to easily distinguish friend from foe.
