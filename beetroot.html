Beetroot

Did you know?

Packed with essential nutrients, beetroots are a great source of fiber, folate (vitamin B9), manganese, potassium, iron and vitamin C.

Grow:

Beetroot prefer to be grown in moist, fertile soil in a sunny spot, but will also thrive in raised beds or pots. Although early sowings can be made from late winter, raising plants can be tricky, so for foolproof beetroot, sow seeds directly into the soil from mid-spring.

Water: You want the soil to be slightly moist. If it's wet, hold off on watering. If it's dry, give them a water!

Food: Feed regularly with a general purpose feed

Harvesting: Depending on variety, beetroot is ready to be picked when the roots are between the size of a golf ball and a tennis ball - this is usually 90 days after sowing. To harvest, gently hold the tops and lift while levering under the root with a hand fork.

Bonus:

In some studies, drinking about 2 cups of beet juice daily or taking nitrate capsules lowered blood pressure in healthy adults. Beet juice may also help your stamina when you exercise.

Recipe: Beetroot Soup

Ingredients:

  • 250g beetroot, grated coarsely
  • 250g tomatoes, halved
  • 1 garlic clove, chopped roughly
  • 1 small onion, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive or sunflower oil
  • 250ml stock
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • feta (optional)
Method:
  1. Place the halved tomatoes in an ovenproof dish. Throw over the garlic and drizzle over half the olive oil. Roast them for 25-30 minutes at 190C/Gas mark 5 until soft and pulpy. Rub through a sieve to remove the skin and pips.
  2. Heat the remaining oil in a pan and sweat the onions for a few minutes until soft. Add the beetroot and the stock and bring to the boil. Season lightly lightly with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  3. Simmer gently for 7-10 minutes until the beetroot is tender. Stir in the tomato puree, transfer the soup to a blender and process until completely smooth.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Reheat the soup until thoroughly hot but not boiling. Divide between warm bowls and crumble over a little feta into each bowl.
  5. Serve with crusty bread. Enjoy!
Source: BBC Good Food
